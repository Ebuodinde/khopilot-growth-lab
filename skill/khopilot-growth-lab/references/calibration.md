# Calibration Discipline

A prediction about what will hold or spread is a **hypothesis, not a fact**, until it is measured against this surface's real data. The engine's rules are only as good as the outcomes that have falsified or confirmed them. Treat every published piece as an experiment that updates the rules.

## Discipline

1. **Predict explicitly.** Before publishing, state the expected behaviour (will it clear the survival gate? which lever — hold or send — is it leaning on?). Write the prediction down so it can be wrong.
2. **Measure the real outcome.** After publishing, record the actual skip rate (% gone by 3s), completion, and sends/reach per piece (raw per-post numbers live in the tenant's `metrics-intake.md`, never in the engine rules).
3. **Falsify, don't rationalise.** When the measured result contradicts the prediction, the rule was wrong, not the data. Do not invent a post-hoc story that saves the prediction.
4. **Abstract the lesson.** Convert what the data taught into a **topic-independent cause→effect rule**, stripped of the subject, the format, and the specific piece. The rule must read as transferable logic, not an anecdote. An example in a rule pollutes it; record the mechanism, not the case.
5. **Update the engine.** Fold the abstracted rule into `system.md` / `hook-engine.md` and supersede any rule it disproves.

## Standing inferences (abstracted, topic-free)

Cause→effect rules earned from measured outcomes. No subjects, no examples — mechanism only. Confidence is low while the sample is small; revise on contradiction.

- Early-skip is driven by openings that are *low-stakes AND mundane AND off the seed audience's niche* — not by resolvedness as such. A fully recognized, "already understood" opening can still clear the gate when it carries high visual/sensory stakes or tight topic-audience relevance. → Hold at the opening has multiple sources (unresolved question, sensory/aspirational pull, niche match); none is individually necessary.
- Relatability tracks sharing more than holding. A relatable concept can still fail the gate; a low-relatability but visually strong, on-niche concept can clear it. → Keep HOLD and SEND distinct; do not assume relatability holds attention.
- A piece that genuinely fails the gate (none of the three hold-sources present) yields near-zero distribution; its ceiling is irrelevant. → Clearing the 3s remains a real precondition; only the *prescription* for clearing it is broader than first assumed.
- **META — the load-bearing one:** theory-based pre-ranking of which concept will hold has not matched measured outcomes; predictions have been wrong in both directions (a projected winner that failed, and a flagged failure that won). → Lower the prior on confident pre-prediction. Demote any single "gate rule" to a risk flag until repeatedly confirmed. Resolve ranking by testing several diverse bets and reading real skip/completion/sends, not by naming a winner in advance.

Append new inferences here as data produces them; keep each one subject-free. When a new measurement contradicts an inference above, rewrite the inference — do not annotate it with an excuse.
