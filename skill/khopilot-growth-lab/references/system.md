# The Rig — Canonical Video System

The fixed structure every video is built on. Topic is the only variable. Master this once; pour any subject through it.

## 1. The two levers (optimize only these)

| Lever | What it decides | How it's won |
|---|---|---|
| **Watch time** (Meta #1 signal) | Whether the post survives its seed test and keeps getting shown | Mute hook clears the <3s skip → retention holds the curve |
| **Sends per reach** (strongest non-follower signal) | Whether it breaks out of followers into the recommendation waves | A built-in *"send this to someone who…"* moment |

Secondary positive votes (saves, comments, likes per reach) help but are not the lever. Pick **one target metric per video** and engineer its trigger.

### HOLD and SEND are different mechanisms — never trade one for the other

- **HOLD** (keeps the viewer watching → watch time → survival) is produced by *unresolved tension*: something in the frame that does not yet make sense.
- **SEND** (makes the viewer share after watching → reach) is produced by *relatability / recognition*: "this is exactly my person/situation."

These act at different times and cannot substitute for each other. Relatability does **not** hold attention — a viewer who instantly recognizes the feeling has nothing left to resolve and leaves. A concept's SEND ceiling is irrelevant until it has cleared HOLD. **Order is fixed: win HOLD first (the gate), then SEND amplifies the survivors.** Never let projected share-potential excuse a weak opening.

## 2. Eligibility hygiene (non-negotiable, or reach is capped)

- **Original** content (reposts/aggregator → non-recommendable).
- **No third-party watermark** (TikTok/CapCut logos get pushed down).
- **Audio present**, **under 3 minutes**, account in good standing.
- Designed **mute-first** (~half of views are silent).

## 3. The survival gate — the most important 3 seconds

The <3s skip is a literal Meta prediction model: it is a **hard filter, not a scored criterion.** A concept that cannot clear it has a ceiling of zero no matter how strong everything downstream is — do not average the gate against other strengths, and do not let a high projected ceiling override a weak opening. Gate first; everything else is for survivors.

Two thresholds, two jobs:
- **1.5s = scroll-stop (psychological).** Raw pattern interrupt. Job: *don't swipe.* Carried mostly by **visual + text**.
- **3s = commitment (algorithmic + psychological).** Job: *commit to watching* by withholding closure.

### What clears the gate: more than one mechanism (none of them necessary)

The opening must *earn* the first 1.5s of muted attention. Measured outcomes show this can come from several independent sources — do not treat any single one as the required gate:

- an **unresolved situation** — a visible element that doesn't make sense yet, an outcome in doubt;
- **high visual/sensory stakes** — a striking, kinetic, or aspirational image that is worth looking at even when fully understood;
- **tight topic-audience relevance** — the seed audience instantly recognizes "this is my thing."

The measured **failure mode is the opposite of all three at once**: a *low-stakes, mundane, off-niche* opening — instantly understood **and** visually flat **and** outside the seed audience's interest. Resolvedness alone is **not** the failure; a fully recognizable scene can still hold if it is visually strong or highly on-topic. (Relatability tracks sharing more than holding — see §1 — but it is not the gate either.)

**Do not disqualify a concept just because its opening resolves into a recognizable scene.** Flag only genuinely low-stakes / off-niche openings, and treat even that as a *risk to test*, not a certainty — theory-based pre-ranking of openings has not reliably matched measured skip rates (see `calibration.md`). The honest mute test for frame 1: *is there a reason to keep looking — a question, a striking image, or my exact niche?* If none of the three, it's a risk; test it, don't trust the verdict.

Win 1.5s and you buy 1.5 more seconds. Win 3s and you buy the whole video. **Design two distinct hits**, not one warm-up.

## 4. The matrix — 5 beats × 3 channels

The real structure is the product of two axes: **beats** (time) and **channels** (modality).

| Beat | Time | VISUAL | AUDIO | TEXT |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1. HOOK** (scroll-stop) | 0–1.5s | pattern-interrupt / "what is this?" frame, motion in frame 1 | spoken first line — 5–10 word promise, no "hey guys"/credentials | 1–7 word bold headline, stands alone on mute |
| **2. LOCK / re-hook** (commit) | 1.5–3s | a visual that opens a loop / raises a question | sharpen the specific curiosity gap | caption confirms the gap |
| **3. BUILD** (value, ~70%) | middle | change something visual every ~3s (cut/angle/zoom/b-roll) | "but / therefore" progression, zero fluff, one idea per line | emphasize the key words |
| **4. PAYOFF** (close loop) | 10–20% | show the result/answer on screen | deliver the promise — no paywall tease | the last line worth sharing |
| **5. BUTTON + LOOP-BACK** | 0–3s | last frame visually echoes frame 1 → invites rewatch (lifts completion %) | the share/comment trigger | CTA matched to the target metric |

**Hook law:** in beat 1, all three channels say the **same** thing (stacking). Sound-off viewer reads it, sound-on viewer hears it, no-reader sees the motion. Redundancy in the hook, divergence after.

## 5. The 4 transferable writing moves

Applied to any topic:

1. **Decide at frame 1.** The hook's only job is to earn the next second (Sugarman "slippery slide", Hormozi). Not clever — *forces the next line.*
2. **Open a *specific* gap.** A precise missing piece, not vague mystery (Loewenstein curiosity gap). "You're doing X wrong and it costs you Y."
3. **Hold by value-per-second + causality.** Cut fluff, one idea per line, "but/therefore" between every two beats (Parker/Stone), a visual reset every ~3s, keep a loop open (Zeigarnik).
4. **Over-pay the loop.** Close it completely and over-deliver until reciprocity carries the CTA (Hormozi reward). Never open a loop you don't close.

## 6. Match the ending to the metric

| Target metric | Ending move | Placement |
|---|---|---|
| **Sends** | payoff feels like "you NEED to show someone this" | at payoff |
| **Comments** | a question / divisive take / "comment X" | plant early (12–18s) for comment velocity |
| **Saves** | "save this for later" + genuine utility | end |
| **Follows** | identity line / "there's more where this came from" | after payoff, never before |
| **Completion %** | loop-back: last 0.5s matches frame 1 | end (the single highest-leverage trick) |

## 7. The factory contract

A reusable video = **a beat×channel matrix with slot-in lines, b-roll, and a metric-matched CTA.** Keep the rig fixed; swap the topic. This is what makes batching real: map the beats once, pour new subjects fast, hold quality constant while scaling output.
