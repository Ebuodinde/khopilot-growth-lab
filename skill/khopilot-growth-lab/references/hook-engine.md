# Hook Engine

Read when generating or auditing hooks. The hook is ~80% of the job: the only beat that decides whether the other 14 cells of the matrix are ever seen. Never write one hook — generate many, score, pick.

## The two-part hook (Hormozi)

`HOOK = [Call-out] + [Promise]`

- **Call-out** — who is this for / what's the topic. The right viewer self-selects ("if you ride…", "every driver who…").
- **Promise** — what you get if you keep watching. Specific > round. Concrete > category.

The hook's *only* job is to force the next second. Test every hook against: **does this make leaving feel like a loss?** If not, it fails — however clever.

## The taxonomy (deck of lenses)

Generate hooks across these types; each exploits a different attention mechanism. (Type-rank from OpusClip's 34,635-clip study; Product/Outcome averaged highest.)

| Type | Mechanism | Reusable rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Result / Outcome showcase** | payoff IS frame 1, zero setup friction | open on the end-state/transformation before explaining |
| **Expert / explainer setup** | credibility → curiosity → promise in 3 beats | authority fast, then a gap, then the promise |
| **Contrarian / myth-bust** | brain can't leave a contradiction unresolved | "Everyone says X. They're wrong." then resolve |
| **Specific-number / listicle** | numbers are involuntary triggers + open a Zeigarnik loop | "7 ways… #1 surprised me." odd, specific numbers |
| **Callout / command** | breaks passive scroll, forces a decision | "Stop scrolling." "If you're X, watch." self-selects |
| **Warning / negativity** | threat-detection bias (losses > gains for attention) | "Don't do X until…" "This is costing you Y." |
| **Curiosity / FOMO** | exclusion aversion + curiosity gap | frame as insider/just-surfaced info they're outside of |
| **Story-in-progress (in medias res)** | drops viewer mid-tension = built-in open loop | start at the most charged moment, backfill later |
| **Visual pattern-interrupt** | novelty/motion caught pre-consciously | unexpected object/motion/angle in frame 1 |

## 3-channel hook stacking

A hook is not one line — it's three simultaneous hooks that say the **same** thing:

- **Visual hook** — stops the scroll before language registers. A "what is this?" frame, motion, a reveal, an unusual angle. Wins the 1.5s.
- **Audio/verbal hook** — the spoken first line, 5–10 words, the promise direct. No intro, no credentials.
- **Text hook** — 1–7 word on-screen headline, bold, readable, stands alone on mute. Often the single most important because the muted majority reads it.

Write all three. If any one channel fully conveys "why keep watching," the hook is robust to *how* the viewer consumes.

## Generate-many: the 70/20/10 split

For each asset, produce a batch of hooks (aim 8–15):
- **70%** re-skins of patterns that already won (proven tension, new angle).
- **20%** winner-adjacent variations.
- **10%** bold experiments.

Then **score and pick the top 3** to test. Don't marry the first.

## Awareness matching

Match the hook's job to where the viewer is:
- **Unaware** → pattern interrupt.
- **Problem-aware** → agitate the pain.
- **Solution-aware** → differentiate (speed/simplicity/unique mechanism).
- **Product-aware** → proof / specific numbers / case.
- **Most-aware** → urgency / scarcity / offer.

## Gate before scoring (a risk flag, not an auto-eliminator)

First apply the survival gate (system.md §3). The opening must earn the first 1.5s of muted attention through **at least one** of: an unresolved question, high visual/sensory stakes, or tight topic-audience relevance. A resolved/recognizable opening is fine if it is visually strong or squarely on-niche. Flag — and prefer to redesign — only openings that are *low-stakes, mundane, AND off-niche* (none of the three). Treat the flag as a risk to test, not a certainty: pre-ranking of openings has not reliably matched measured skip (see `calibration.md`). HOLD is a prerequisite for distribution, but which opening clears it is decided by data, not theory.

## Scoring rubric (rank the survivors)

Among hooks that pass the gate, score each 0–2; keep the highest total:

1. **Clarity** — topic legible in <1.5s, on mute?
2. **Self-selection** — does the right viewer feel "this is for me"?
3. **Specific gap** — precise missing piece (not vague mystery)?
4. **Send potential** — would someone DM this to a specific person? (the breakout lever — a SEND property, only counts once the gate is cleared)
5. **Payoff credibility** — does it credibly promise a resolution worth staying for?
6. **Channel independence** — does it survive with sound off / with only the text?

## Kill a hook if

- The opening is low-stakes AND mundane AND off-niche — no question, no visual pull, not the audience's thing (the measured gate-failure pattern). A recognizable scene that is visually strong or on-niche does NOT fail.
- Topic isn't clear in 2 seconds.
- It could belong to any generic account in the niche.
- It's a hollow wrapper ("POV:", "let me explain", "you won't believe").
- It opens a loop you can't pay off.
